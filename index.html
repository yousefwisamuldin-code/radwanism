The Radwanic Supremacy

Overview

The Radwanic Supremacy The Radwanic Supremacy (sometimes stylized as Redwanic, and rarely as Rodwanic) is a political ideology founded by Radwan Al-Kibbani’s loyal friend, Yousef Wisam. Its core vision is of a hungerless world a realm where the ancient sin of gluttony is no longer condemned but celebrated as a virtue of strength and fulfillment. Radwanics believe that Radwan himself is destined to become the supreme ruler of all restaurants, the unchallenged sovereign of sustenance. The movement’s history is marked by its notable rivalries. Chief among Radwan’s adversaries is Ahsan, a self-proclaimed “cool” figure whose numerous followers, though loyal, are considered by Radwanics to be too ignorant and technologically primitive to create something as monumental as this site. Yet Ahsan is not the only opposition in Radwan’s saga. His complex frenemy, Ibadullah, an ambitious manipulator who once believed that the peasants of the world were unworthy of food, reserving it all for himself. His reign of selfish indulgence persisted until Yousef, the most devoted of Radwan’s allies, confronted him and ended his hoarding. Though Ibad’s betrayal left its scars, forgiveness triumphed. Today, he remains both Radwan’s and Yousef’s companion tolerated, if not fully trusted. The bond between them is fragile but enduring, a living reminder that even gluttony has room for redemption. Radwan Al-Kibbani, born in 2004 in Tripoli, Lebanon, is widely recognized for his legendary appetite and his most famous achievement: consuming 103 chicken wings in a single session at the Wings Club. The event, now known as The Great Devouring, marked the foundation of the Radwanic Supremacy and solidified his place as the symbol of boundless hunger. As of the present day, the number of Radwanics continues to grow at an unprecedented rate, their ideology spreading across friend circles, group chats, and digital enclaves one bite at a time.

Foundational Beliefs

Food is not scarce. People are just stingy.

  • Hunger is an enemy of the state.
  • Snacking is a human right.
  • Gluttony is not immoral — it is enthusiasm.
  • Buffets require strong centralized leadership.
